# Lateral Thinking Pattern Bank

This reference distills recurring moves from the 390-row lateral-thinking corpus in the shared Google Sheet and adapts them to Doppl case studies. Use it as a checklist, not a lookup table.

## 1. Question The Named Unit

Many lateral answers work because the noun in the question is not the unit that matters.

- The long jump record mattered inside the pentathlon, not the standalone long jump.
- A price ending in 99 cents was about penny circulation, not retail psychology alone.
- Falling publisher traffic is about the click disappearing, not rankings disappearing.
- Airport baggage complaints are about passive standing time, not objective elapsed time.

Ask: What is being counted, sold, delayed, improved, or protected? Is that the real unit?

## 2. Redraw The Boundary

The trick often hides in where the boundary starts or ends.

- A broadcast day can start at 6am and run to 29:59.
- A bridge can connect two pre-existing churches rather than explain why churches were added to a bridge.
- A yacht perimeter includes the water column, not only the deck.
- A market shift can include hidden downstream dependents, not only the visible industry.

Ask: What boundary is assumed: time, place, jurisdiction, perimeter, category, market, object, or lifecycle?

## 3. Treat The Environment As The Mechanism

The solution may be embedded in physical or environmental affordances.

- Loaded dice reveal themselves by floating.
- Cast iron patterns must account for shrinkage.
- A plastic football bottle can focus sunlight like a lens.
- Loft insulation fails because the loft is physically full.

Ask: What would matter if this were a physical workflow rather than a belief problem?

## 4. Change The Signaling Surface

Sometimes the cheapest intervention changes what people see, infer, or feel.

- VanMoof's TV box made handlers treat bikes as fragile.
- Heinz's red label edge made counterfeit ketchup visibly off.
- A process map makes A&E waits legible.
- A distorted transit map can redistribute route choice.

Ask: Which surface does the deciding actor actually look at?

## 5. Move Upstream Of The Bottleneck

Many answers work before the obvious failure point.

- Ask passengers about liquids before the gray bins.
- Detect a drone kilometers away, then change the scene before filming range.
- Shift MRT riders before peak load forms.
- Clear a loft before the installer arrives.

Ask: Where is the last cheap moment before the failure becomes expensive?

## 6. Solve For The Hidden Actor

The named beneficiary is often not the behavior-controlling actor.

- White Castle solved landlord leverage, not just restaurant design.
- VanMoof solved package-handler behavior, not bicycle durability.
- Hospital signage solved patient uncertainty and staff safety, not wait duration.
- Price endings helped a penny newspaper by changing trader/customer coin flow.

Ask: Whose behavior, leverage, or constraint actually controls the outcome?

## 7. Convert A Constraint Into A Feature

The answer may use an annoying constraint as the mechanism.

- Cash-only DEF CON protects attendees from traceability.
- A "419 + 1" hotel sign avoids theft of a "420" sign.
- A visible countdown makes traffic or waits tolerable.
- Starship's cost collapse makes imagination, not launch, the scarce input.

Ask: What constraint can become a filter, signal, affordance, or moat?

## 8. Use Social Meaning Instead Of Force

Some solutions change meaning rather than material conditions.

- A royal potato field made potatoes desirable.
- A warning, status challenge, or competence cue can outperform another sign.
- A movable restaurant changes the meaning of lease negotiation.

Ask: What status, shame, pride, scarcity, ownership, or identity signal is active?

## 9. Find The Disappearing Event

For zeitgeist cases, look for the event that vanishes.

- If autonomous vehicles remove crashes, crash-dependent sectors reprice.
- If autonomous cars obey by construction, traffic stops and fines lose substrate.
- If answer engines satisfy intent, the click stops being the unit of distribution.
- If GLP-1 mutes reward, impulse occasions disappear across categories.

Ask: What event, behavior, transaction, or risk used to happen routinely but may no longer happen?

## 10. Track The Uneven Edge

The future rarely arrives everywhere at once.

- Autonomous mobility appears metro by metro.
- Answer engines hit evergreen informational content before breaking news.
- Firm-power constraints matter first where AI demand outruns interconnect queues.

Ask: Who experiences the new regime first, who is stranded outside it, and what arbitrage forms at the boundary?

## 11. Prefer Small Mechanisms With Large Leverage

A good lateral answer often feels simple after the frame changes.

- Add clearing labor, not more persuasion.
- Add a color comparison, not enforcement.
- Add a cue song, not drone warfare.
- Add movable buildings, not better rent negotiation.

Ask: What tiny operational change attacks the actual constraint directly?

## 12. Falsify The Frame

Before committing, try to break the answer.

- If the proposed mechanism would work only by luck, it is not enough.
- If it solves the surface symptom but not the recovered problem, downgrade it.
- If it violates a hard constraint, cap feasibility.
- If it has no measurement path, add one or mark confidence lower.

Ask: What observation would prove this frame wrong?
