# Case Study: The Unevenly Distributed Future (Autonomy's Adoption Asymmetry)

## Summary

Autonomy arrives as a patchwork, not a wave: geofenced, metro-by-metro,
regulation- and mapping-gated. So it lands first and hard in a few places and not
the rest, opening three asymmetries — a perception split (believers vs. those who
don't think it's real), an intra-national divergence (served vs. lagging metros),
and a first/third-world split (with a leapfrog wildcard). The story is the gulf
itself: "the future is already here, just not evenly distributed." This version
withholds the synthesis for use as a Doppl `zeitgeist_synthesis` prompt.

## Problem

### Statement

Autonomy is real and deploying. Given that it arrives as a geofenced patchwork, what
are the consequences of its *uneven distribution*?

### Background

AV services are hyper-local and geofenced, launched city-by-city and gated by
mapping, regulation (state-by-state), weather, and politics. Most people have never
ridden one and many doubt it works; a minority live in it daily. Deployment is
concentrated in rich-country metros, though mobile/financial leapfrogging shows
adoption can invert expectations in specific emerging markets.

### Why It Matters

If the regime change is described as a uniform average ("autonomy is coming"), the
asymmetries — where the actual winners, losers, arbitrage, and political fault lines
are — go unseen.

### Current State

Coverage focuses on whether autonomy works and which company leads, not on the
distributional gulf its rollout creates.

### Impact

Mispricing the asymmetry blindsides the unserved/disbelieving and misses the
arbitrage and migration toward served zones.

### Scope

The distributional (where/when/for-whom) effects of autonomy's patchwork rollout.
Excludes the specific sectoral unwinds (sibling cases).

## Failed Attempts

> Consensus plays the thesis must beat.

### "Autonomy Is Coming"

**Approach:** State the average arrival.

**Outcome:** True but empty; hides the distribution.

**Why It Failed:** The asymmetry is where the value and the fault lines are.

**Lesson:** Describe the gulf, not the average.

### "It Will Roll Out Everywhere Soon"

**Approach:** Assume a fast, uniform wave.

**Outcome:** Ignores geofencing, mapping, regulation, weather, politics.

**Why It Failed:** Deployment is structurally a patchwork.

**Lesson:** It's a patchwork, and the patchwork is the point.

### "Rich Places Get It First, End Of Story"

**Approach:** Assume one-way concentration.

**Outcome:** Misses leapfrog inversions (mobile/M-Pesa pattern).

**Why It Failed:** Adoption can invert where legacy systems and regulation are
light.

**Lesson:** Hold the international angle two-sided.
