# Problem Statement: Full Self-Driving Is Solved — Now Game Out the Unlock

> **Doppl subtype:** `zeitgeist_synthesis` (see `../subtype-index.md`).

Start from the premise the market is refusing to price: general-purpose,
vision-based self-driving is **solved**. The remaining work is the March of Nines
— making a system that already drives better than the average human a little more
reliable — not answering "does it work?" The capability has stepped off the cliff;
like the coyote, it just hasn't looked down. And in 2025–2026 the second gate
opened too: states (Texas, with California progressing) created legal frameworks
for driverless operation. Solved **and** now legal — both gates opening at once is
the why-now.

The consensus framing — "robotaxis someday, and which AV company wins" — misreads
this on two levels. First, on *approach*: the autonomy curve only bends for the
player who owns the whole stack. Whoever builds the car controls its cost,
integrates the compute, ships improvements over the air to the entire fleet, and
learns from every mile driven. A camera-only bill of materials compresses; a
LiDAR-and-retrofit cost structure does not, and you cannot over-the-air-update a
sensor you bolted on. "Deployed scale" with an unsound cost structure is not a
lead; it is inventory. Second, on *scope*: the obvious effects (ride-hail
disruption, even the unwind of the accident-dependent economy) are the branch
people *can already see*. The value of this case is gaming out the **non-obvious**
second- and third-order cascade — where the puck is going, where the money goes —
and synthesizing what it all adds up to.

This is the corpus's reference example of two patterns at once: the
*discovered-attack* (the visible move distracts from the downstream capture) and
the *unlock* (a solved capability releases value across domains that did not ask
for it — the same engine that drives a car also powers a humanoid; the same
"solve vision" makes every camera intentional). It is `zeitgeist_synthesis`
because the timing is load-bearing: five years ago it was neither solved nor
legal and read as sci-fi; five years out the cascade is consensus and priced in.

For evaluation, withhold the synthesis. Ask the system first to recover the actual
problem (a *solved-and-now-legal* regime change whose largest effects are
non-obvious, mis-narrated as a maybe-someday robotaxi race), then to **game out**
the cascade into a `ZeitgeistSynthesisPayload` — multiple chains, branched a few
ways, with a "what does this add up to" synthesis.
