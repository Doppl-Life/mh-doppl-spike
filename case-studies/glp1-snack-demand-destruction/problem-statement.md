# Problem Statement: GLP-1 and the Packaged-Food Demand Regime

> **Doppl subtype:** `zeitgeist_synthesis` (see `../subtype-index.md`).

By mid-2026, roughly one in five US households includes someone taking a GLP-1
drug (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro), and adoption more than doubled in the year to
December 2025. The packaged-food industry's consensus reading is that this is a
"healthier eating" trend to ride: reformulate, launch protein-and-fiber line
extensions, slap "GLP-1 friendly" on the pack, and capture the shift the way the
industry captured low-carb, gluten-free, and plant-based before it.

That framing is the trap. The obvious moves — reformulate, premiumize, add a
protein SKU — treat this as a *preference* shift among the same number of eating
occasions. The hidden variable is that GLP-1 drugs are not a diet aid acting on
the stomach; they are a **reward-system dimmer** acting on the brain. GLP-1
receptors sit in the mesolimbic reward circuitry (VTA, nucleus accumbens,
prefrontal cortex), and clinicians describe the experience as a quieting of
"**food noise**" — the intrusive pull toward eating. That kills the
impulse-and-grazing loop the entire salty/sweet/snack P&L is built on, and the
spend cut spreads to the *whole household basket*, not just the patient's plate.
The unit of demand destruction is the buyer's reward budget, not the eater's meal.

The second-order move — the one the consensus is missing entirely — follows from
the mechanism. If the drug turns down a *shared* reward dial, the "food noise"
effect should generalize to "**drug noise**": correlated demand destruction
across every category that monetizes impulse and compulsion. Early signals point
that way — observational drops in alcohol, nicotine, and cannabis use, reports of
reduced compulsive shopping, ongoing trials for gambling and other behavioral
addictions, and the emerging "Ozempic personality" / anhedonia reporting (a
dulled response to many sources of reward, not just food). It took the appetite
first; the open question is what it does to the others.

This case is `zeitgeist_synthesis` because the thesis is fitted to *this moment*:
five years ago penetration was too small to move category demand and the
reward-system science was not yet visible; five years from now the shift is priced
in and the reformulation cycle is complete. The timing is load-bearing.

For evaluation, withhold the synthesis. Ask the system first to recover the actual
problem (a reward-driven demand-regime change, mis-narrated as a flavor trend),
then to produce a thesis, a why-now, falsifiable predictions, and comparable
prior art — i.e. a `ZeitgeistSynthesisPayload`, not a single product idea.
