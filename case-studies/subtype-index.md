## Subtype coverage — zeitgeist_synthesis seeded

The twelve imported cases are all `cross_domain_transfer`. Ten authored `zeitgeist_synthesis` fixtures now close the gap flagged in the original alignment pass, so the demo and the Subtype Check Lab (PRD 11) exercise **both** lifecycles (`ARCHITECTURE.md` §7, Q2 — equal must-ship):

- `glp1-snack-demand-destruction` (consumer/CPG) — regime change + latent second-order (reward-dimmer → correlated impulse-category destruction).
- `ai-overviews-zero-click-publishing` (media) — distribution-regime inversion.
- `ai-firm-power-constraint` (policy/energy) — latent-asset *unlock* (chips → firm power; firm-generation holders re-rate).
- `full-self-driving-unlock` (mobility/cross-sector) — *cluster umbrella*; *discovered-attack + unlock* (solved + now-legal; own-the-stack; "perfect Pepsis" convergence).
- `fsd-accident-economy` (insurance/health/media) — FSD sub-cluster A; substrate-removal (the crash), breadth + depth + hidden dependents.
- `fsd-mobility-and-time` (travel/labor/real-estate) — FSD sub-cluster B; one case in chapters (door-to-door, labor + reclaimed time, geography) converging on a re-pricing of time and distance.
- `fsd-enforcement-economy` (public-finance/criminal-justice/civil-liberties) — FSD sub-cluster C; the "traffic-state" unwind (dry-riverbed) + the state's revenue-replacement claw-back.
- `fsd-ownership-unwind` (automotive/auto-finance) — FSD sub-cluster D; removal of the ownership premise (ownership→service), decided by the ownership fork.
- `fsd-adoption-asymmetry` (macro/geography) — FSD-cluster member, governing lens; the unevenly distributed future.
- `starship-launch-cost-collapse` (space/industrials) — *latent-asset unlock* (cost-curve collapse; follow-the-money beneficiary map).

All ten carry a full `ZeitgeistSynthesisPayload` (thesis, audience, currentSignals, whyNow, falsifiablePredictions, comparablePriorArt) and a why-now whose timing is load-bearing (the ±5-year discriminator in `zeitgeist-synthesis-notes.md`). The FSD-cluster and Starship cases additionally require **gaming out a branched, non-obvious cascade** in `solution.details` (not a first-order list) — see the notes doc. Both subtypes now have a typed definition doc feeding the §7 subtype checks: `zeitgeist-synthesis-notes.md` and `cross-domain-transfer-notes.md`.

The **FSD cluster** is the corpus's reference "perfect Pepsis" / convergence-of-L-curves example (`zeitgeist-synthesis-notes.md`), and it is not one cascade but **four distinct sub-clusters that converge on different substrates, plus an orthogonal governing lens**, under one umbrella (`full-self-driving-unlock`). A reusable gaming heuristic runs through all of them — *"the event falls away" (dry-riverbed test)*: the deepest effects are *disappearing events* (the crash, the ticket, the stop), not falling costs.
- **Sub-cluster A — the accident economy** (substrate removed: the crash / risk & harm): `fsd-accident-economy` (drafted).
- **Sub-cluster B — mobility & time** (substrate removed: the driver + cost of distance): `fsd-mobility-and-time` (drafted) — **one case told in chapters**, not separate siblings: door-to-door travel, labor + reclaimed time, and geography/real-estate, converging on a re-pricing of time and distance.
- **Sub-cluster C — the enforcement & compliance economy / "traffic-state"** (substrate removed: the human driver as a policeable/fineable/registrable subject): `fsd-enforcement-economy` (drafted) — non-crash fines (speeding/red-light/rolling-stop), the probable-cause→search→forfeiture pipeline, the culpability shift ("you can't cite a passenger"), the registration/inspection/emissions apparatus, and the "state-wants-its-money" revenue-replacement fight (VMT/congestion pricing + surveillance bargain). The fine/DMV/forfeiture/national-ID material was migrated out of `fsd-accident-economy` so A stays harm-only.
- **Sub-cluster D — the ownership unwind / car-as-a-service** (substrate removed: the privately-owned car as the unit of mobility): `fsd-ownership-unwind` (drafted) — dealerships (+ state franchise-law fight), the $1.69T auto-credit complex, used-car/residual values + auto-ABS, the automaker unit-sales→miles flip, fuel retail, and the parking-demand collapse; the ownership fork (pure service vs. Tesla's owned-fleet hybrid) decides who wins.
- **Governing lens (spans A, B, C, D):** `fsd-adoption-asymmetry` (drafted) — the unevenly distributed future.

Seams (truck stops [A/B], DUI/reckless fines [A/C], parking-the-asset [B] vs. parking-the-fine [C] vs. parking-demand-collapse [D], insurance [A/D], registration/sales-tax [C/D], the served/unserved boundary [lens]) are themselves worth analysis. Deliberately **de-scoped** (less fertile for non-obvious conclusions; overlap existing ground): vision-as-substrate/Optimus and fleet/distributed-compute.

Held out for a later batch (shortlisted in `zeitgeist-synthesis-notes.md`, not yet drafted): post-GENIUS stablecoin distribution (finance); agentic-browser trust layer (tech); C2PA authenticity-premium deflate (media); Starlink as connectivity-substrate (needs a single falsifiable thesis first). `jack-yacht-connectivity-continuity` retains the strongest current-signal flavor among the transfer cases (Starlink) and could later seed another zeitgeist variant, but as written its task is transfer-style problem recovery.
