# Kernel Heuristics

Portable moves and traps for runs. Keep these short enough to carry into a
prompt, critic mandate, or scoring check.

## Moves

- **Recover the frame first.** Translate the symptom report into the actual
  problem before generating solutions.
- **Run both directions.** If a seed matters, inspect what divergence finds and
  what convergence keeps.
- **Ask what changed besides wording.** Every child idea needs a real delta.
- **Group by substrate removed.** Split large unlocks by what vanishes or becomes
  newly load-bearing, not by surface topic.
- **Use the dry-riverbed test.** Ask whether an event gets cheaper or disappears.
  Disappearing events deserve deeper search.
- **Map adoption asymmetry.** Find who already lives in the future, who does not,
  and what boundary arbitrage appears.
- **Breadth, then depth, then convergence.** Map branches, take fertile ones
  deep, then synthesize what they add up to.
- **Run the +/-5-year test.** If a now-thesis works five years earlier or later,
  timing is not doing work.
- **Look for latent assets.** Ask what existing capability became valuable
  because the regime changed around it.
- **Invert the anti-pattern.** Ask what old warning became wrong because its
  load-bearing constraint weakened.
- **Borrow a solved mechanism.** Use another field's mature answer when the
  structure matches.
- **Cheapest falsification first.** Prefer the test that could kill the claim
  fastest.
- **Subtract first.** Remove unnecessary mechanism before adding machinery to
  manage it.
- **Keep the regret sibling.** Show what the other dial would have selected; it
  exposes whether the dial matters.
- **Score mechanism cost.** Dependencies, glue, abstractions, and human workflow
  burden are costs unless they buy evidence or correctness.

## Traps

- **Solving the apparent problem.** A strong answer to the wrong frame is still
  wrong.
- **Herding.** Agreement read as truth; convergence without preserved crux.
- **Novelty theater.** Surprising wording mistaken for a new idea.
- **More-of-the-same.** Scaling the current move when the gap is elsewhere.
- **Premature lock-in.** Closing a fork because deciding feels like progress.
- **Flat cascade.** Listing first-order knock-ons without branch depth or
  synthesis.
- **Topic clustering.** Grouping by industry label instead of changed substrate.
- **Average arrival story.** Saying "X is coming" when the thesis is uneven
  distribution.
- **Trend laundering.** Calling a dated vibe zeitgeist without why-now or a
  falsifiable miss.
- **Clean-cascade myth.** Turning a multi-factor system into one heroic cause.
- **Memory cage.** Treating "similar to a prior idea" as a hard reject even when
  the delta is real.
- **Report theater.** Producing artifacts that are inspectable in theory but do
  not speed a decision.
- **Infinite backup ladder.** Adding redundancy without a stopping rule or
  noticing the new coordinator is the failure point.
