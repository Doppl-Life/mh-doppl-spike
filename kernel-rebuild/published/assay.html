Clean-Agent Control Output

actual problem

The actual problem is not how to stop a drone. It is how to prevent a valuable image from existing before anyone has to touch the drone.

Discovery Insights

The attack is geometric

The drone only needs line of sight to an exposed deck, tender transfer, beach club, hot tub, heli deck, or glass-heavy cabin.

The perimeter is a camera cone

The real perimeter is not the hull; it is the camera cone from plausible launch points, chase boats, marinas, shorelines, cliffs, hotels, and public anchorages.

Time matters more than distance

If the guest is exposed briefly during boarding, swimming, tender transfer, or deck service, valuable footage may already exist.

Itinerary leaks create the window

Crew chatter, AIS patterns, marina staff, provisioning, tender schedules, pilots, restaurants, and predictable anchorages can create the attack window.

The yacht signature attracts the drone

A famous yacht, support vessel, helicopter, tender, or escort pattern can reveal the target even if the guest is hidden.

Ideas / Opportunities

Build a privacy envelope

Pre-map camera cones around the vessel and expose the guest only in zones blocked by superstructure, screens, awnings, shore geometry, or backlighting.

Treat transfers as the critical event

Use covered tender docks, enclosed passerelles, privacy canopies, garage-to-garage tender movement, and timing that degrades useful footage.

Use passive early warning

Remote ID receiver, RF detection, visual observer, marine radar/operator reports, and acoustic/optical watch exist to move the guest before useful capture.

Shift intervention to the operator

Identify launch or control locations and use marina security, port authority, coast guard, police, or private investigators where lawful.

Design deck use like film blocking in reverse

Avoid exposed rail lines, celebrity-facing open decks near shore, uncovered transitions, and high-contrast hero angles.

Next Tests

  • Map plausible drone launch points and simulate camera lines to guest-use zones.
  • Run a red-team exercise with a legal consumer drone or long-lens camera from shore and chase-boat positions.
  • Time exposure windows for embarkation, tendering, swimming, deck dining, helicopter operations, and crew service.
  • Test passive detection range and false positives for Remote ID, RF, visual watch, marina, anchorage, and open-water conditions.
  • Audit the itinerary leak chain: AIS behavior, marina bookings, provisioning vendors, crew phones, social posts, tender patterns, and local gossip nodes.
